Project Idea:

Overview

I’m not too sure of an appropriate project to focus on, most of my background is in business Information technology and interfacing applications and data together. However, one area that has fascinated me the concept of independent thinking and automating a device. I wonder if it is possible to create a system where the real battle robots of Robot Wars or Battlebot can be controlled using software rather than the currently used remote controls and would it be effective.  

Motivation

The source of this interest comes from two parts of my background. I used to play a MS DOS game called Robowar that enabled you to program a theoretical robot to hunt and destroy some opposition in a virtual arena. The code was essentially C based with simplified sensory and movement commands. The specific limitations built into the game created a scenario where the complexity of using commands reduces the ability and speed of reaction of the robot. The other part of my thought is based on the television series Robot Wars and Battlebots where mechanical “robots” are controlled by remote control to fight to disable their opponents in a controlled, but inimical environment with obstacles and impediments that changes over the duration of the combat.

Description

This project would require an amount of hardware which should be minimal to determine a proof of concept. We would need the following equipment:-

•         Processing power, say a Raspberry Pi

•         Sensor facilities, camera or infrared or ultraviolet detector

•         Stable and expandable roving platform.

•         Power supply or battery pack

The budget for this should be very small, say in the order of a couple of hundred dollars initially.

There are available devices available for education purposes that should make most of this hardware function to the intended level to provide a platform. Where the work is needed is to write, test  and implement software that could provide a proof of concept for this project.
It is not the intention of this project to initially provide an exhaustive programming and test environment but it could be done later.

The Robot should be able to move independently and be able to sense and make relatively sensible decisions to move while avoiding obstacles.Tools and Technologies

I believe that this could be done relatively cheaply with one of my existing Raspberry pi’s and the purchase of a cheap compatible robot chassis. Investigation shows that the hardware could be had for a couple of hundred dollars which should provide enough in the way of “robot” to enable a proof of concept. To avoid the accusation of plagiarism, we may need to avoid using the standard language of the raspberry Pi, i.e. Python.

Outcome

There is no way to do a full study of this, I imagine that it could be the thesis for a full Doctorate to do properly, but it should be possible to do a limited study of the ability to sense, decide, and control a device in a limited way to test whether this could work. I would be happy that the robot could trundle around without running into walls or falling off the edge of a platform, while still avoiding other obstacles in a controlled manner.
